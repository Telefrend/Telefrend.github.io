WHAT WE OFFER
For smallholder farmers in Uganda, the agricultural value chain is informal and dis-aggregated, hindering the flow of information and resources. According to the 2013 Policy Paper on transforming smallholder farming to modern agriculture in Uganda by the National Planing Authority, agriculture in Uganda is dominated by smallholder farmers who occupy the majority of land and produce most crops, livestock and fisheries products. They contribute approximately 80% of the food supply and yet, they are producing only a fraction of their potential. Smallholder farmers produce far below their potential yields, often due to poor access to extension services and market.

Farmers who need to access extensions services have to travel miles and as well depend on a series of middlemen to get their produce to market because they have little bargaining power. Development agencies and buyers on the other hand have to rely on personal contacts to reach these farmers and this limits their operations to specific areas.

Cukwa is an interactive voice response (IVR) system platform that links individuals in the agricultural value chain to farmers in the last mile. Our platform helps farmers learn about best practices, give them somewhere to turn when they have questions about their crops, and connect them to a marketplace where they can locate supplies and find buyers.

Our platformâ€™s use-case is simple, users dial a mobile number and by pressing some button on the phone and following voice prompts, they choose a service of their interest and get connected to available extension service providers, buyers or farmers.

The system is designed as a private, easy-to-use platform to link farmers, buyers and extension service providers in real time. Everyone has an option to record over a phone call their service or need that will later be played back to callers who can help or want help.

Cukwa is a robust interactive voice response system platform built on a remote cloud server and accessible through a mobile number, just like most call centers do, enabling farmers to get access to extension services and market in real-time, and in their local language.
